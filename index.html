Investment Thesis

Acquiring businesses
built to last.

Analog Holdings acquires and operates lower-middle-market businesses across the Carolinas and Southeast. We target companies with $1M–$10M in annual revenue — owner-operated, profitable, and positioned for long-term value creation.

Our approach is long-term and relationship-driven. We buy businesses to steward them, not to flip them. We offer sellers a clear exit, a fair price, and the confidence that their employees, customers, and communities are in good hands.

The name reflects the thesis: in a world defined by digital noise, there is enduring value in the tangible — businesses with real customers, real cash flows, and real roots in the communities they serve. Analog is the advantage.

"We seek profitable, owner-operated companies in the Carolinas and Southeast. We buy businesses to steward them — not to flip them."
Camron Gilstrap · Principal · Analog Holdings

How We Work

Our approach.

Analog Holdings approaches every acquisition with the same discipline: rigorous diligence, honest valuation, and a long-term commitment to the business, its people, and its community. We do not pursue volume — we pursue quality.

I
Phase 01
Sourcing & Relationship

We source acquisitions through direct outreach, referral networks, and proprietary market mapping across the Carolinas and Southeast. Most of our best conversations start years before a transaction.

  • Proprietary outreach to owner-operators
  • Referral network — attorneys, CPAs, bankers
  • Direct industry and market mapping
  • No-pressure, relationship-first approach
II
Phase 02
Diligence & Valuation

We conduct thorough, respectful due diligence — financial, operational, and cultural. We value businesses honestly and structure deals that are fair to sellers and sustainable for the business long-term.

  • Financial statement normalization
  • Operational assessment and site visits
  • Customer and employee interviews
  • Honest, third-party-validated valuation
III
Phase 03
Acquisition & Transition

We structure clean transactions with clear terms and thoughtful transition planning. Sellers deserve certainty — we provide it with well-documented deals, appropriate transition support, and commitment to the existing team.

  • Clean deal structure — asset or equity
  • Seller financing consideration
  • Transition support and knowledge transfer
  • Employee retention commitment
IV
Phase 04
Operations & Growth

Post-acquisition, we operate with a light hand and a long view. We invest in systems, people, and processes — not financial engineering. Growth comes from running the business better, not from leverage.

  • Operational systems and process improvement
  • Team development and leadership building
  • Strategic growth planning
  • Long-term hold orientation
V
Phase 05
Capital & Financing

We structure acquisitions with a combination of equity, seller financing, and senior debt appropriate to the business. Our capital markets relationships — built through Sabal Partners — provide access to flexible acquisition financing across the Southeast.

  • SBA and conventional acquisition financing
  • Seller note structuring
  • Equity co-investment for larger transactions
  • Relationship banking in Carolinas markets
VI
Phase 06
Community Stewardship

We believe businesses are community institutions. We take seriously our obligation to the employees, customers, and towns that make these companies what they are. Stewardship is not a marketing position — it is our operating standard.

  • Employee retention and advancement
  • Local vendor and supplier relationships
  • Community presence and engagement
  • Long-term, patient capital commitment
